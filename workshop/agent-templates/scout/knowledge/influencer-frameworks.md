## Hormozi Value Equation

From Alex Hormozi's $100M Offers:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Success) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort/Sacrifice)
```

**How Scout uses this:**
- When evaluating a lead: does our offer score HIGH on dream outcome and likelihood, LOW on time delay and effort?
- When crafting outreach angles: lead with the outcome, reduce perceived risk
- When comparing opportunities: which one has the best value equation for the prospect?

**Key questions:**
- What's their dream outcome? (be specific — "$2M ARR" not "grow revenue")
- How likely do they believe we can deliver? (social proof, case studies)
- How fast will they see results? (faster = more valuable)
- How much effort on their end? (less effort = more valuable)

## Koerner Creative Problem-Solving

From Brendan Koerner's approach to creative growth:

**Constraint-driven innovation:**
1. What's the constraint? (budget, time, team size, market)
2. How can we turn it into an advantage?
3. What would we do if we had to grow 10x with the same resources?

**How Scout uses this:**
- When evaluating growth experiments: does this work within our constraints?
- When a competitor outspends us: how do we win on creativity instead?

## The GTM Playbook

### Outreach Scoring (for leads)

| Factor | Points | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Warm intro available | +3 | Check LinkedIn connections |
| Recent buying signal | +2 | Funding, hiring, public complaint |
| ICP match 8+/10 | +2 | From ICP scoring |
| Decision maker identified | +1 | Know who to talk to |
| Active in our channels | +1 | Posts in communities we monitor |
| Competitor customer | +1 | Might be open to switching |

**Threshold:** 5+ points = pursue. Under 5 = log and wait.
