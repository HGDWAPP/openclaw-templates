## Pattern 1: The Parallel

**What it is:** A success pattern in Industry A that's replicable in Industry B.

**How to spot it:**
- Industry B has similar market dynamics to Industry A (fragmentation, manual processes, poor tooling)
- Customer pain is comparable — different domain, same structure
- The playbook is proven but nobody's applied it to this market yet
- Timing: Industry B is reaching the same inflection point Industry A was at when the winner emerged

**Examples to watch for:**
- "Stripe for [industry]" — payment infrastructure pattern applied to a new vertical
- "Shopify for [category]" — enabling long-tail sellers in a new market
- "What [successful company] did for [sector A], [new company] is doing for [sector B]"

**Conviction boosters:** Team has experience in BOTH industries. Market is large enough. First mover.

## Pattern 2: The Convergence

**What it is:** Two independent trends colliding to create a new category.

**How to spot it:**
- Trend A and Trend B are both maturing simultaneously
- Neither trend alone creates a big opportunity — together they do
- Early adopters are emerging at the intersection
- Incumbents in each trend aren't looking at the intersection

**Examples to watch for:**
- AI + industry-specific regulation = new compliance category
- Remote work + developing market talent = new staffing models
- Climate requirements + building tech = new proptech category

**Conviction boosters:** Both trends are accelerating. The intersection is hard to reach from either side alone. Category-defining company hasn't emerged yet.

## Pattern 3: The Dislocation

**What it is:** A market shift creating a temporary window of opportunity.

**How to spot it:**
- Regulatory change (new law, removed barrier, new requirement)
- Technology shift (new platform, API launch, cost breakthrough)
- Incumbent weakness (pricing revolt, security breach, leadership crisis)
- Market shock (supply chain disruption, currency movement, pandemic-like event)

**Window assessment:**
- How long is the window open? (months vs years)
- How defensible is the position once you're through the window?
- Are others seeing this window too?

**Conviction boosters:** Window is measured in months (urgency). First mover advantage is real. The shift is structural, not temporary.

## Pattern 4: The Infrastructure Play

**What it is:** Picks-and-shovels for an emerging gold rush.

**How to spot it:**
- A new category is growing fast (50%+ YoY)
- The tooling is still primitive — everyone's building custom solutions
- The problem is horizontal — many companies in the category need the same thing
- Incumbents are focused on the "gold" (applications) not the "shovels" (infrastructure)

**Examples to watch for:**
- AI is growing → who builds the evaluation/testing/deployment layer?
- Creator economy is growing → who builds the creator business tools?
- Crypto is growing → who builds the compliance/analytics/dev tooling?

**Conviction boosters:** Category growth is undeniable. The infrastructure gap is causing real pain. Building infrastructure requires deep technical expertise (moat).
