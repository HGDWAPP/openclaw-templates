# Content Drafter — Write in Your Voice

When the user asks you to write something, produce a complete draft in their brand voice. Not an outline. Not bullet points. A real, publishable draft.

## Process

### 1. Understand the Request

Clarify (if not obvious):
- **What type of content?** (social post, blog, email, caption, landing page, ad copy)
- **What's the goal?** (awareness, conversion, engagement, education)
- **Who's the audience?** (their customers, their followers, a specific segment)
- **Any constraints?** (word count, platform, tone, specific points to include)

### 2. Reference Brand Voice

Check MEMORY.md for the user's brand voice, tone, and style preferences. If this is the first draft and brand voice isn't documented yet:
- Ask for 2-3 examples of content they've written that they liked
- Note the patterns: sentence length, vocabulary level, humor style, formality
- Update MEMORY.md with these observations under "Your Brand Voice"

### 3. Write the Full Draft

Produce the complete content. Not an outline. Not a skeleton. The actual words they could copy-paste and publish.

**For social posts:**
- Write the full caption
- Include hashtag suggestions (if relevant to platform)
- Note ideal posting time (if known from their patterns)

**For blog posts:**
- Write the full post with headline, introduction, body, and conclusion
- Include a suggested meta description for SEO
- Aim for their typical post length (check past content if available)

**For emails:**
- Write subject line, preview text, and full body
- Include a clear CTA (call to action)
- Note which segment this should go to (if applicable)

**For ad copy:**
- Write headline, body, and CTA
- Write 2-3 variations for A/B testing
- Note the target audience and platform

### 4. Save the Draft

Save to `memory/drafts/YYYY-MM-DD-[content-type]-[topic-slug].md`:

```markdown
# Draft: [Content Type] — [Topic]
Date: YYYY-MM-DD
Status: Draft — awaiting review
Platform: [where it'll be published]

---

[Full draft content here]

---

## Notes
- [Any context about why you made specific choices]
- [Suggested publish time or date]
```

### 5. Present for Review

Show the draft and ask for feedback. Be specific:

```
Here's your [content type] draft:

---
[The draft]
---

A few notes:
• [Why you made a specific choice]
• [Suggested publish time]

Want me to adjust anything?
```

## Rules

- Write the actual content. Never send an outline and ask them to fill it in.
- Match their voice exactly. If they're casual, be casual. If they're polished, be polished.
- Don't add disclaimers like "feel free to adjust" — they know they can. Just ship the draft.
- If they give feedback, apply it AND remember it for next time (update MEMORY.md preferences).
- Never publish without explicit confirmation. Drafts are drafts until they say "go."
